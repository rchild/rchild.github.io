How to be Danish

  1. Hej!

    Saying "hello" is an anouncement that you are not a viking. Most Danes can tell anyway just by looking at you, but you can still confuse them by saying "Hej!" instead of your usual dull "hello". --Note the capitalisation and exclamation mark. It's pronounced like that too.

    You are the queens official sandwich maker

    Slapping piles of food between slices of bread might be fine where you come from, but Danes are sculpting tiny works of art when it comes to the union of bread, deli and cucumbers. It's actually rather baffling to watch the proficiency of every Dane at the buffet table while they delicately arrange milimetre thin slices of tomato atop a crescendo of cold meat and cheese. I suspect that they secretly inspect and critique eachothers handiwork as well. Why else would it be so important to perfect these works of intricate food art?

    Ride a bicycle

    Cars are for haters of mother earth and lovers of parking meters. And walking is far too, well, pedestrian. You must be able to handle a bicycle hands free whilst moving your entire family around in the shopping basket without breaking a sweat or violating any traffic laws. If you cannot do this, you must be German.
